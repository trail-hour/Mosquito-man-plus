Mosquito Season Timeline in Durham Region

Mosquito activity in Durham Region typically starts building in late May as temperatures warm up and standing water from spring rain sticks around. Pressure climbs through June, peaks during the hot, humid stretch of July and August, and tapers off through September as nights cool down. That timeline tracks fairly closely with public health mosquito surveillance in the region, which generally runs from mid-June into September.

Tick Season Timeline: A Longer Risk Window

Ticks follow a different curve. They tend to become active earlier in spring than mosquitoes, often as soon as temperatures stay consistently above freezing, and they can remain active into late fall on milder days, well past when mosquito activity has dropped off. This gives ticks a longer overall risk window than mosquitoes, even though peak activity for both tends to overlap during the warmer months. Wooded edges, tall grass, and conservation-area-adjacent properties in Clarington, Uxbridge, and Scugog see this most, since that's the habitat ticks prefer year-round.

Why Timing Treatment Matters More Than Reacting

Both pests build their populations over the season rather than showing up all at once. For mosquitoes, adults live a few weeks but eggs remain dormant in soil until conditions are right, so a yard's population rebuilds continuously through summer. For ticks, life stages progress across the season, with different life stages posing different levels of risk at different points. In both cases, starting prevention or treatment before pressure builds is far more effective than trying to knock down an already-established population mid-summer. Our mosquito control services include a tick control add-on for exactly this reason, timed alongside the mosquito program rather than as an afterthought.

Booking Around the Season, Not After It Starts

Homeowners in Oshawa and across Durham Region who book in late spring, before populations build up, are generally dealing with a lighter starting point than those who call in for a fix once bites are already a daily problem in July. The flip side is worth knowing too: a warm, wet fall can extend activity past the "usual" end of the season, and a particularly wet spring can pull the start date earlier for both pests. If you're planning around an event or just want the season handled before it ramps up, a quote request now is timed better than waiting.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does mosquito season start in Durham Region?

Mosquito activity typically starts building in late May, climbs through June, and peaks during the hot, humid stretch of July and August before tapering off by late September.

When does tick season start and end?

Ticks tend to become active earlier than mosquitoes, often as soon as temperatures stay consistently above freezing in early spring, and they can remain active into late fall on milder days, giving them a longer overall risk window.

Why does timing matter more than reacting to bites?

Both mosquitoes and ticks build their populations over the season rather than appearing all at once. Starting prevention or treatment before pressure builds is far more effective than trying to knock down an already-established population mid-summer.

Can a wet spring or warm fall shift the season?

Yes. Year-to-year weather is the biggest variable. A wet spring can pull the mosquito season's start date earlier, and a warm, wet fall can extend activity past the usual end of the season.

When should I book treatment relative to the season?

Booking in late spring, before populations build up, generally outperforms waiting until bites are already a problem in July, since it's easier to keep numbers down early than to knock down an established population later.
