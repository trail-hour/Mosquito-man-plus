What's Actually in a Professional Mosquito Spray

This is one of the most common questions Durham Region homeowners ask before booking a first treatment, and it's a fair one to ask. Professional mosquito treatments use products registered with Health Canada's Pest Management Regulatory Agency (PMRA), the body that sets approval and labeling standards for pest control products used in Canada. Mosquito Man Plus uses EPA/PMRA-registered products across its residential, event, and commercial treatments for that reason, applied by trained technicians who follow the product's approved application method.

A barrier treatment targets specific resting sites, like shaded shrubs and fence lines in a Whitby backyard, rather than blanketing the whole property, which limits how much product ends up on open lawn areas where kids and pets actually play.

Re-Entry Time: How Long to Wait

In practice, most professional barrier sprays need roughly 30 minutes to fully dry on plant and grass surfaces before the treated area is considered dry to the touch. Once dry, the active ingredient is bound to the treated surface rather than airborne, which is why the standard advice is to keep the area clear only until it dries, not for the rest of the day.

Crawling infants are a reasonable exception to that general timeline, since they have more direct skin and hand-to-mouth contact with treated grass than an adult walking across it. Waiting a full hour before letting a crawling baby on treated lawn is a sensible extra margin.

Extra Precautions for Cats, Dogs, and Crawling Infants

Dogs generally tolerate treated areas well once dry, and the standard re-entry window covers most of the precaution needed for them. Cats are more sensitive to some active ingredients used in yard treatments than dogs, so keeping them indoors a bit longer than the standard window is a reasonable extra step, even though the concentrations used are well below toxic thresholds.

A few simple habits round this out: bring pets inside during application, remove food and water bowls from the yard beforehand, and give the treated area its full dry time before anyone (two-legged or four) heads back out. Our mosquito control services follow this same approach on every visit.

Natural and Low-Impact Alternatives

If pet or child safety is a bigger concern for your household, it's worth mentioning when you request a quote, since scheduling, treatment focus, and any extra precautions can be discussed up front rather than as an afterthought on treatment day. Natural and organic alternatives exist too, though they generally offer a shorter window of effectiveness than standard synthetic barrier treatments, which is a trade-off worth weighing against how much mosquito pressure your yard actually sees.
